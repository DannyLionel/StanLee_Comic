Stan Lee was born on December 28, 1922, in Manhattan, New York City

He married Joan Clayton Boocock on December 5, 1947.

His daughter Joan Celia "J. C." Lee was born in 1950.

He has previously written obituaries for newspaper, and press releases for the National Tuberculosis Center.

During WW2, Stan Lee enlisted in the army, where he repaired communications equipment.

He transferred over to the Training Film Division, where he wrote manuals, slogans, training films and cartoons. He was one of only 9 men given the title of playwright.

He served with children's book writer and illustrator Theodor Geisel, later known to the world as "Dr. Seuss."

He co-created Iron Man, Hulk, Thor, the X-Men, Daredevil, Doctor Strange and the Fantastic Four.

His most successful character is Spider-Man.

Many films based on his characters have been produced, and are very succesful. The Marvel Cinematic Universe has grossed over $21 billion!

He has played a cameo in every single marvel film produced.
